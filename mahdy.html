Abnormalities in Shape
▼
Gemination
Partial division of ONE enamel organ → 1 tooth looks like 2; NORMAL tooth count; single root canal
Fusion
Union of TWO adjacent enamel organs → involves dentin; REDUCED tooth count by 1; usually 2 root canals
Concrescence
Post-developmental; 2 teeth joined by cementum; trauma/crowding; complication: both extracted together
Dilaceration
Sharp bend in long axis; usually trauma during development; most common = max central incisors
Taurodontism
Enlarged pulp chamber; failure of Hertwig sheath; 3rd molars most, 1st molars least
Dens invaginatus types
I: crown confined; II: below CEJ, blind sac; III: perforates root (most dangerous)
Cusp of Carabelli
Palatal surface of mesiolingual cusp of upper 1st molar
Talon cusp
Extra cusp on lingual cingulum of anterior tooth; upper permanent teeth; has pulp extension
Dens evaginatus
Central groove of premolars; mandibular teeth; enamel + dentin + pulp extension

Welcome! These are high-yield exam pearls and mnemonics I use to help students ace this chapter. Pay special attention to the comparison tables — they're the most tested.

🔑 The Golden Rule: Gemination vs Fusion

  • Gemination: ONE tooth tries to divide → tooth COUNT is NORMAL → single root canal
  • Fusion: TWO teeth join → tooth COUNT is REDUCED by 1 → two root canals
  • Memory trick: "Fusion = Few teeth"; "Gemination = G for Greater count"
  • Both involve dentin, both usually affect incisors, both in deciduous or permanent dentition

💡 AI (Amelogenesis Imperfecta) — Exam Favorite

  • Hypoplastic = THIN but HARD enamel (matrix defect = quantitative)
  • Hypocalcified = SOFT, enamel MORE radiolucent than dentin
  • Hypomaturation = SOFT, enamel SAME radiolucency as dentin
  • X-linked dominant hypoplastic: females show "lyonization" (alternating bands) → classic MCQ trap
  • All affect BOTH dentitions (hereditary) → key differentiator from acquired defects

✅ Fluorosis — Critical Numbers to Memorize

  • Disease starts at ≥1.5 ppm
  • 2-6 ppm: calcification defect (mottled enamel) — matrix normal
  • >6 ppm: matrix formation starts to fail (pitted enamel)
  • >8 ppm: deciduous teeth affected + skeletal sclerosis
  • Deciduous teeth usually spared because fluoride is sequestered by maternal skeleton
  • Mottled teeth are MORE resistant to caries (calcium fluoroapatite)

⚠️ High-Yield Comparisons for MCQs

  • Hereditary defects → affect ONE dental tissue (enamel OR dentin), BOTH dentitions, ALL teeth
  • Acquired defects → affect BOTH enamel AND dentin, ONE dentition (usually permanent), SINGLE/GROUP of teeth
  • Hereditary defects → vertical/random pits; Acquired → HORIZONTAL pits (chronological)
  • DGI vs DD-II: DGI affects both dentitions equally; DD-II has primary teeth like DGI but permanent teeth show thistle-tube pulps

🧠 UV Fluorescence — Easy 2 Marks

  • Tetracycline → YELLOW fluorescence under UV
  • Porphyria → RED fluorescence under UV
  • This is a classic exam question — never confuse these two!

💡 Hutchinson's Triad for Congenital Syphilis

  • 1. Hutchinson's incisors (barrel-shaped, notched — "screwdriver")
  • 2. Ocular interstitial keratitis
  • 3. Eighth nerve deafness
  • Plus: Moon's molars (dome-shaped), Mulberry molars (irregular tubercles)
  • Caused by Treponema pallidum; teeth affected = upper permanent incisors + first molars

✅ Taurodontism — Quick Notes

  • From Greek: "taurus" = bull + "odonto" = tooth
  • Enlarged pulp at expense of roots
  • 3rd molars MOST affected; 1st molars LEAST affected
  • Pathogenesis: Hertwig root sheath fails to invaginate at proper level
  • Radiograph: rectangular tooth outline; reduced/absent cervical constriction
  • Associated with Klinefelter syndrome and amelogenesis imperfecta types IVA/IVB

⚠️ Don't Confuse — Common MCQ Traps

  • Dens invaginatus ≠ Dens evaginatus: invaginatus = folded IN; evaginatus = cusp popping OUT
  • Anodontia ≠ Oligodontia: anodontia = ALL missing; oligodontia = 6+ missing
  • Mesiodens = maxillary anterior (not mandibular)
  • Concrescence = cementum joining; Fusion = dentin joining (must involve dentin)
  • Turner's tooth = ONE or TWO teeth (acquired localized hypoplasia from local trauma/infection)
